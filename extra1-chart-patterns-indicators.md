# Extra Notes — Chart Patterns & Indicators (Deeper Technical Analysis)

Expands [Phase 1](phase1-strategies.md). These are the visual patterns and indicators that traders layer on top of the 4 core strategy archetypes to confirm signals and time entries/exits more precisely. Patterns are probability tools, not guarantees — treat the "success rates" below as historical tendencies, not promises.

---

## 1. Reversal Patterns (signal a trend may be ending)

- **Head and Shoulders** — three peaks: a left shoulder, a higher middle peak (the head), and a right shoulder roughly matching the left. A "neckline" connects the two troughs between the peaks; a close below the neckline signals the uptrend is reversing into a downtrend. (Inverse head-and-shoulders = the bullish mirror image, signaling a bottom.) One of the more statistically reliable reversal patterns.
- **Double Top / Double Bottom** — price hits a resistance level twice without breaking through (double top, bearish reversal) or a support level twice without breaking down (double bottom, bullish reversal). Confirmation comes when price breaks the level between the two peaks/troughs.

## 2. Continuation Patterns (signal the existing trend is pausing, then likely resuming)

- **Flags & Pennants** — a sharp price move (the "flagpole") followed by a brief, tight consolidation (the "flag" — parallel channel, or "pennant" — small symmetrical triangle). Typically resolves by continuing in the flagpole's original direction.
- **Triangles** — periods of consolidation with converging trendlines:
  - **Ascending triangle** — flat resistance, rising support → often resolves upward (bullish).
  - **Descending triangle** — flat support, falling resistance → often resolves downward (bearish).
  - **Symmetrical triangle** — both lines converging → direction-neutral until it breaks; watch volume on the breakout (same volume-confirmation rule from Phase 1's breakout strategy applies here).
- **Rectangles** — a horizontal trading range bounded by clear support/resistance; a lower-reliability pattern that benefits from confirmation with RSI, MACD, or volume before trusting the eventual breakout direction.

**Practical note**: continuation and reversal patterns are really just the visual shape of what's happening underneath — consolidation before a breakout (Phase 1, Strategy 3) or a trend running out of steam. Learning to name them helps you communicate and recognize setups faster, but the underlying logic (support/resistance + volume) is the same thing you already know.

---

## 3. Fibonacci Retracement

- After a significant price move (up or down), Fibonacci retracement levels mark possible points where price might pause or reverse before continuing in the original direction.
- Key levels: **23.6%, 38.2%, 61.8%, and 78.6%** of the prior move.
- Common use: in an uptrend, wait for a pullback to the 38.2% or 61.8% level as a potential entry for continuing the trend, rather than chasing price at new highs. Combine with support/resistance from Phase 0 — a Fib level that lines up with a prior support zone is a stronger signal than either alone.

---

## 4. MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

- A momentum indicator built from two EMAs (typically 12-day and 26-day) plotted as the "MACD line," plus a 9-day EMA of that line (the "signal line"). The **histogram** shows the gap between the two.
- **Bullish signal**: MACD line crosses above the signal line (histogram flips positive).
- **Bearish signal**: MACD line crosses below the signal line (histogram flips negative).
- **Divergence** — when price makes a new high/low but MACD doesn't confirm it (e.g., price hits a higher high while MACD makes a lower high) — this is a classic warning sign that the trend's momentum is fading, even though price is still moving in the same direction. Often used alongside Fibonacci levels: MACD confirming a bounce at a Fib retracement level adds confidence to the setup.

---

## 5. Putting It Together — How These Layer on Phase 1

None of these replace your Phase 1 strategies — they're confirmation tools:
- **Trend-following**: MACD crossovers can double-check your MA crossover signals; Fibonacci retracements give you better pullback entries than just buying at the market.
- **Mean-reversion**: reversal patterns (double top/bottom) reinforce RSI/Bollinger Band signals at extremes.
- **Breakout trading**: triangles, flags, and rectangles ARE the consolidation patterns you're watching for — apply the same volume-confirmation rule from Phase 1 before trusting the breakout direction.

**Caution**: stacking too many indicators leads to "analysis paralysis" and conflicting signals. Pick 1-2 confirmation tools per strategy, not five.
